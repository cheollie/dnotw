a date night out of this world

The goal of DNOTW is to have space data (MR occurrences, plasma behavior, solar winds) accessible in a visible format, while also intriguing more users with creative ‘regular day-use’ implications. Made for space enthusiasts who want to have a galactic date, or someone curious about the visualization and sonification of an aspect of space on their birthday!

Features

three steps to build your perfect date night. (imagine a calendar input for a date here). source code linked at the bottom through the github logo.

the date

learn about the magnetic reconnection events that occurred or will occur on your date, and what it means (the effects on space weather which impacts satellites and power systems; potential northern light sightings)! this information is generated by analyzing the vector components of interplanetary magnetic field data and solar wind plasma particle data from the Geotail, ACE, and Wind satellites.

the gift

want a spacey gift unique for your special day? solar wind prediction data from WSA-Enlil visualized with the enlilviz Python module used to make pretty patterns. currently, it looks like a latte art which would be cute on a coffee, but ideally it should have looked like a flower like Bridgman’s gorgeous visualization. (if given more time, the user can customize the colors of the image!)

the music

set the ambiance for your date night ✨. spotify curated a playlist of songs that are out of this world. (if given more time, this would have been a sonogram of plasma data, unique to the day requested, and adjusted to the right vibe.)
